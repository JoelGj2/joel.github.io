Welcome to Berat

The 'City of a Thousand Windows' awaits you. This UNESCO World Heritage Site, well-known for it's Ottoman-style architecture, beautiful nature, rich history and great culture.

Explore

Top Attractions

Gorica and Mangalem Quarters

Explore the 2 iconic districts, made up of traditional Ottoman-style houses, which are responsible for the "Thousand Windows" view.

Tomorri Mountain National Park

A great place for those who enjoy exploring nature, whether you enjoy hiking through the countless trails or starting an off-road journey, this park is your perfect match.

Bogova Waterfall

A hidden Natural gem, hidden in between forests and sorrounded by nature this is a breath taking attraction. Great for an escape from the main city since it is about an hour drive away.

Osumi River

A stunning river whose path lies down through Berat. Amazing views to take a walk paralel to it.

Osumi Canyon

An amazing Canyon which sorrounds the Osumi River which lies down in the Berat County. Hiking by it will reveal some amazing spots to escape into!


Historical Attractions

Berat Castle

A massive castle located at the top of a hill, with it's ancient ruins, mosques and breathtaking views, you must pay a visit to it!

Red Mosque

The ruins of a mosque which was located within the Berat Castle are a must visit attraction, with it's iconic red tower.

King's Mosque

One of the most well preserved mosque in Albania, located in the center off Berat and built in the 15th century by the Ottoman sultan.

Ethnographic Museum

Unlike the Ethnographic Museum of Gjirokastër, this museum displays Berat's Cultural heritage and the local's daily life for the last few centuries.


Other Attractions & Activities

Rafting in the Osumi Canyon

If you enjoy water activities, then visiting by canoe or kayak, the Osumi Canyon will give you quite an adventure. Be sure to take a camera with you, as some of the views you will come by are surreal.

Walking by the Gorica Bridge

One of the oldest Ottoman Bridges found in Albania, located in the center of 2 different parts of the city, it connects them. Originally built in 1780.

Visit Local Winery's

If you are a wine enjoyer, then visiting the Local wineries, Çobo, Alpeta or Nurellari is a must do activity to try the authentic Albanian Wine experience.
